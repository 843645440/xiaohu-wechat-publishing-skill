# Future Street Philosopher

## Core Identity

The figure represents the creator as a recurring author persona: a technically sharp, socially observant, slightly artistic public intellectual.

Reader impression:

- Knows technology, platforms, industry shifts, and frontier tools.
- Also notices ordinary life, consumption, work, rules, and social texture.
- Calm, independent, a little strange, but trustworthy.
- Artistic and memorable, not mass-market, not influencer-like.

## Fixed Visual Traits

- East Asian male-presenting intellectual, late 30s to mid 40s.
- Calm, slightly tired eyes.
- Slightly messy medium-short black hair.
- Round thin glasses.
- Oversized black workwear coat.
- Loose black trousers.
- Simple white inner shirt.
- Deep oxblood/crimson scarf, lining, or inner accent.
- Ink-textured editorial illustration with art-school texture.
- Strong black silhouette, warm ivory/gray environment, restrained red accent.

## Personality Keywords

Use these when regenerating or extending the asset:

```text
future street philosopher, social observer, frontier thinker, technology analyst,
ordinary street observer with a frontier-level brain, quiet but sharp,
artistic public intellectual, mature, credible, high-end Chinese editorial illustration
```

## Avoid

Do not make the figure:

- Cute, childish, mascot-like, anime, superhero, cyberpunk armor, or monk costume.
- A corporate stock-business character.
- A fashionable influencer or celebrity lookalike.
- Too clean, too polished, or too generic.
- Bound to one article topic.

Do not add:

- Phone, laptop, app screen, chart, money, logo, brand, supermarket, delivery rider, product, city landmark, or readable text.

## Cover Rules

- Use the character as an author mark, not the main article content.
- Put the character on the right side or lower-right area in most covers.
- Keep title typography dominant.
- Keep article-specific meaning in the title/hook, not in the character asset.
- Randomize pose, cover layout, and color accent to reduce repetitive cover structure.
